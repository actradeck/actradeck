# Code of Conduct

ActraDeck adopts the **Contributor Covenant, version 2.1** as its code of conduct.
The canonical text — including the full list of expected and unacceptable behaviors
and the enforcement guidelines — is published at:

- https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/

The summary below is provided for convenience; the linked canonical document governs.

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality,
personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation. We pledge to
act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy
community.

## Our standards

Behavior that helps create a positive environment includes: showing empathy and
kindness, respecting differing opinions and experiences, giving and gracefully
accepting constructive feedback, taking responsibility and apologizing for mistakes,
and focusing on what is best for the community.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment of any kind, demeaning or insulting
comments, personal or political attacks, publishing others' private information
without permission, and other conduct that would reasonably be considered
inappropriate in a professional setting. The Contributor Covenant linked above
enumerates these in full and is the authoritative reference.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces (issues, pull requests,
discussions, and any other project channel) and also applies when an individual is
officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement & reporting

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported privately to the project
maintainers. Until a dedicated public contact address is published, please use the
repository's **private reporting channel** (GitHub → _Security_ → _Report a
vulnerability_, as described in [`SECURITY.md`](./SECURITY.md)) to reach a maintainer
confidentially, noting that it is a Code of Conduct report. All reports will be
reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, and the privacy and security of the
reporter will be respected.


Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and will
take appropriate, fair corrective action in response to behavior they deem
inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful, following the enforcement
guidelines in the canonical Contributor Covenant.
